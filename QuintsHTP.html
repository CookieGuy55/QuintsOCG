How to play

Quints is a back and forth card game where you want to be the first person to get 3 cards in your bride zone

There are 5 zones you can play your cards in

And there are 3 types of cards

  • Characters
  • Episodes
  • Events

You can play 1 Character and 2 episodes each turn, and as many events as you are able to

You get a bride in your bride zone by capturing one of the Futaro cards, "Get yo man" as Luke says

To capture a Futaro card you must match or beat the power of the Futaro card

Once you have captured a Futaro card, you put the character you attacked with into the bride zone, while discarding all attached cards, and do the effect of the Futaro card

When you attack a Futaro card, your opponent has an opportunity to play events from their hand to weaken your attack. You can play events in responce to this and this goes on until someone wins or loses the lane

You can attack more than one Futaro card per turn, so your opponent has to choose the right ones to stop

How to start the game

Play rock paper scissors to decide who goes first

The winner of rock paper scissors goes first and the match proceeds with their Futaro deck

Draw 5 cards for your starting hand, you get 1 mulligan (Put the 5 cards back in your deck, reshuffle and draw 5 new cards)

Note: You do NOT draw any cards in the first start phase of the game (Player 1's first turn = No start phase draw)

Types of cards

Characters

You can tell if a card is a character by the text in the top right that says [Character]

Characters have a Character power in the top left

Characters have an Episode power in the bottom left for when they are set to another character

Characters also can have a chunk of text below the image which are the abilites of that character

Example

Episodes

Episodes can be identified by the text in the top right that says [Episode]

Episodes only have an Episode power in the bottom left for when they are set to another character

Episodes can not end up in the bride zone unless stated on the card

Episodes also can have a block of text below the image which are the abilites to follow if you meet the conditions

Example

Events

Events are identified by the text in the top right that says [Event]

Events do not have power and can not be played as Characters or set as Episodes

Events are powerful because of their abilities that are in the block of text below the image
